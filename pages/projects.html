Project on fairness in LLM based recommendation systems

Q1 2024 – Q2 2024

With the rise of AI assistants and automated systems in our daily lives; it is crucial to examine their accuracy and fairness. The line between personalized user feeds and false biased recommendations can be thin, but important. In this project, Rozhan explored the extent of gender bias in Large Language Models used in recommendation systems, highlighting how such biases can negatively impact user experience
I examined how transformers work and explored ways to reduce their biases. My focus was on evaluating fairness in large language models (LLMs) and then training them accordingly. My goal was to enhance the existing LLM, improve the code, and retrain the model to make it less biased towards certain characteristics.
I found this project to be a valuable opportunity to apply my skills to a critical societal issue: the accuracy of our online feeds. It provided me with hands-on experience, allowing me to build components from scratch and plan the project scope and timeline more efficiently.
